C — Split: Dark Archived
v1 Design Notes

A — Dark Darkroom: The current direction, refined. Amber safelight glow, dot grid texture in notes. Immersive but can feel oppressive on bright days.


B — Cream Notebook: Ruled-line paper texture in notes, off-white background. More readable in daylight. Dark contact sheet thumbnail pops against the light bg. Moleskine energy.


C — Split: In-progress rolls feel open and alive (white, dot grid, orange film-stock status chip). Archived rolls are dark and dense — permanent, stored. The app literally changes personality as a roll ages.


D — High Contrast B&W: No amber — just black and white. Roll number printed in white-on-black block at top of notebook (like a label on a film canister). Most radical, most distinctive.

V2 — Cream · Dot Grid · Colour · Bottom Bar Options
Cream background with dot grid (not ruled lines). Three bottom bar options. Colour accent exploration — should we use status colours, a single accent, or none?

v2 Notes — Cream + Bottom Bar

Dot grid vs ruled lines: Dot grid (radial-gradient 22px) feels more like a Leuchtturm bullet journal than a composition notebook. Less loud, more flexible.


Bottom bar 1 (plain text): Cleanest. Status as text label, date on the right. Doesn't shout. Works for all statuses identically.


Bottom bar 2 (colour band): The colour band makes the status immediately scannable from across the room. Amber/cyan/orange each have distinct emotional tones that match the status. Most visually expressive.


Bottom bar 3 (status in header): Cleanest overall — the notes area is completely uninterrupted from header to nav. But you lose the ambient status awareness the bar gives.


Bottom bar 4 (left border): The colour runs alongside your writing — subtle but always visible. Works well with the dot grid since the grid has no left margin. A bit like a journal margin rule.


On colour generally: The status colours (amber/cyan/orange) are earned — they track the physical journey of the roll. Without any colour, the app feels like a receipt printer. A single accent (like the current amber) feels arbitrary once the LAB/FRIDGE distinction matters. The status colours are the right call if we use any colour at all.

V3 — Nav & Actions Rethink
Keeping the cream + dot grid + coloured status band. Rethinking the bottom nav (feels too app-like, too busy) and the ••• (too generic). The notebook should feel immersive — you shouldn't feel like you're in an app.

v3 Notes — Nav & Actions

Nav 1 (no nav on notebook): The most immersive. You're in the roll — the coloured band is the entire bottom. Back arrow exits. This pairs best with Nav 4 on the home screen (text tabs). No icons anywhere.


Nav 2 (text labels only): Works on both home and roll detail. Still clean without icons. The underline active state is typographic and intentional.


Nav 3 (floating pill): Playful but might feel heavy in this context. Better for apps with more content types. Probably too "app-y" for a notebook.


Action 1 (band = action): Elegant — one surface, two jobs. Tap band → advance status. But could be confusing: is it a display or a button?


Action 2 (explicit button in band): Clearest UX. "→ Fridge" leaves no ambiguity. Band is still ambient status, but the button is obviously tappable.


Action 3 (next step in header top-right): Tasteful. Amber underlined text in the corner is discoverable but not intrusive. Works well if edit is via long-press or separate gesture.


Action 4 (pull-up handle): The most "native" pattern. The drag handle suggests there's more below — pull up for edit roll, move to fridge, etc. Requires the user to know the gesture exists.

v4 Notes — The cohesive system

Nav on lists only: Home, Archive, Stats, Settings all have the text-tab nav. Any detail view (roll notebook, archived roll, camera, film) has no nav — back arrow exits. This makes detail views feel like separate spaces, not just another tab.


The FAB (+): Stays on all list views. Not on detail views. When inside a roll, "new roll" isn't the relevant action.


Pull-up action sheet: The dark sheet (#1a1a1a) over the cream notebook creates a strong contrast moment — you've surfaced the machine, briefly, to take an action. It feels intentional. The handle on the band invites the gesture without labelling it.


"Edit" top-right: Present on the notebook as plain text, not a button or icon. Dim (#bbb) so it doesn't distract from the notes. Clear enough to find when you need it.


Archive detail view: When you tap a roll in the archive, it opens with back arrow only (no nav), same as the notebook. The archived roll view (contact sheet + timeline + notes) doesn't need the nav either.

v5 Notes

Sheet A (aged paper): Warmest. Still a clear mode shift but it doesn't feel like a different app. Like turning to the back cover of the notebook.


Sheet B (status colour): The roll's identity follows you into the action sheet — amber for loaded, cyan for fridge, orange for lab. Might be too saturated but very distinctive.


Sheet C (frosted cream): Most seamless. Stays in the same visual world completely. Risk: might not feel distinct enough — like the overlay didn't quite arrive.


Nav A ("+ Load" inline): Treats loading a roll as a primary nav action, always visible. Amber makes it scannable. Simple.


Nav B (Load in header): Keeps the nav pure — it's only for navigation, not actions. "Load Roll →" in the page header is contextually correct on the home screen. Cleanest separation of concerns.


Nav C (bordered button): The most distinct — the button shape sets it apart from navigation labels. Split layout (nav left, action right) has a newspaper masthead quality.


Nav D (dots as separators): Everything inline, dots as rhythm. Very typographic. "Load" floats in the center of the bar without needing a shape.

v6 Notes — Final Direction

Notebook: No nav bar. The whole screen is the page. Status band is the only UI element — amber, cyan, or burnt orange. The drag handle sits on the cream strip above home indicator.


Action sheet: Sheet A wins — warm aged paper #2c2420. The roll identity (number + status) in the sheet header means you always know what you're acting on.


Nav: Nav C hybrid — left tabs, amber "+ Load" right, no border. Tabs underlined active. Lives only on list views (home, archive).


Push/pull stepper: [−] · value · [+]. The range is −3 to +3 — a text input is wrong for this. Zero shows as "—" or is empty.


Group headers: "▪ Loaded ────" where the dot is the status colour (amber/cyan/burnt orange) and the rule is the border colour. Status is the only place colour appears in the list.

v7 Notes

Edit form grouping: Three sections (Roll / Timeline / Details) keep the field list scannable without burying dates. Section dividers are thin rules with small-caps labels — same language as the rest of the app.


Date fields: "Set" in amber is the primary CTA when a date isn't set. When set, ✕ appears to clear it. Tapping "Set" expands the row inline (amber left border, slightly warm bg) rather than opening a separate sheet — feels more like a form than a wizard.


Lab field: Expanded inline includes both the date and the lab name text input — they're logically one event, so they live in the same expanded row.


Camera/film picker: Pull-up sheet with search at top, list below, "Add new" always pinned at the bottom. Current selection has an amber ✓. This replaces navigating to a separate route.


Add new camera/film: Navigates to a new page (back → returns to picker). Format and type are segmented selectors (filled dark = active), not dropdowns. Keeps the form keyboard-friendly.
